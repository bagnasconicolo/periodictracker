PeriodicChiCubo - Usage Guide

Welcome to PeriodicChiCubo—your all-in-one platform for managing your personal elemental collection! This guide will walk you through every feature of the website.

1. Getting Started

  • Login/Registration: At the top of the main page you will find fields for username and password. Enter your desired credentials and click “Login & Load.” If you are new, your account is created automatically.
  • Once logged in, your username is displayed and your personal data is loaded. You can also update your profile later on the “Profile Settings” page.

2. The Periodic Table Interface

  • The main page displays an interactive periodic table. Each element is represented by its symbol; hover over an element to see its name and a brief description.
  • Click an Element: Clicking an element opens its details panel where you can update its status (such as Pure, Representative, Alloy, or Wish), add descriptions, and upload an image.
  • Use the Quick Status mode (toggle checkbox and dropdown) to quickly assign a status without opening the details panel.
  • You can also enable the Gallery View to display element images as backgrounds on the table.

3. Editing Your Collection

  • In the details panel, you can update an element’s status, description, quantity (in grams), and purity (in percentage).
  • You can upload an image for an element either by entering an image URL or by using the file upload (max ~2MB). The image will appear in the preview area and is clickable to view full size.

4. Managing Samples

  • For each element, you may add sample images—for example, close-up photos or different views. In the samples section of an element’s details panel, click “Add Sample” to open the sample form.
  • In the sample form, specify the sample’s status, description, quantity, purity, and either an image URL or upload a sample image file. Sample images appear to the left of the text and are clickable.
  • If you have previously added samples, Edit and Delete buttons appear next to each sample (visible only to you). Use these to update or remove samples.

5. Comments

  • You can post comments on any element. The comment section is always visible.
  • Important: To post a comment you must be logged in. If you are not logged in, an inline login form appears right in the comment section.
  • Once logged in, you can type your comment and click “Post Comment.” In the comments list, non‑"Guest" usernames are clickable. Clicking a username opens their public profile info (which shows their collection stats and achievements).

6. Profile & Settings

  • The “Profile Settings” page allows you to update your display name, email, bio, and profile image. You can also change your password here.
  • Your public profile (accessible by other users) displays your achievements and collection statistics (the counts of Pure, Representative, and Alloy statuses).

7. Media Library

  • The “Media Library” page shows all your uploaded images. You can view, delete, and manage your media files.

8. User Directory & Changelog

  • The “User Directory” page lists all public profiles along with summary statistics for each user’s collection.
  • The “Changelog” page provides a history of updates and improvements to the website.

9. Additional Features

  • Achievements: As you add or update your collection, you earn badges (e.g. “All Alkali Metals”, “All Noble Gases”, “First 10 Elements”) that are displayed in your public profile.
  • Sticky Periodic Table: Use the “Stick Table” button to fix the periodic table on the screen while you scroll.
  • Gallery View: Toggle gallery view to have your element images displayed as backgrounds on the table.

10. Navigation

  • Use the top menu links to navigate between pages: User Directory, Profile Settings, About, Usage Guide, Media Library, and Changelog.
  • Click “Go Back to Main” at the bottom of this page to return to the main periodic table interface.
